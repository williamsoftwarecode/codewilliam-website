About Me

A Masters of Engineering (Electrical) student at the University of Melbourne

The unquenchable passion for mathematics and interest in technology has driven me to pursue a career in electrical, electronic, and software engineering. As a final year student, I have but touched the tip of the iceberg of such a vast and diverse field with such boundless potential. This coupled with my unceasing desire to learn and apply my knowledge propels me to continue doing what I love.

In 2014, I graduated with a Bachelor of Science (Mechanical/Electrical Systems) after 2.5 years of study (overloaded for 4 semesters to catch up by half a year). Prior to that, I was studying my Cambridge A-Levels in Taylorâ€™s College.

Currently, I am the Vice President of the Melbourne University Electrical Engineering Club (MUEEC). Previously, I held the position of Secretary of the International Engineering Student Society (IESS), also affiliated with the University of Melbourne.

My interests are in programming, mathematics, reading, gaming, and exercising. I code and script in C, Java, Matlab, Bash, and Perl. I greatly enjoy teaching others as I find it thrilling to impart my experiences and bring people to a better understanding of a topic.

Last Ones Standing Startup Pitch Competition 2016 - 1st Place Winner
Malaysians of Melbourne University (MOMU)
August 2016
Working as a team of engineers with diverse backgrounds, we pitched a startup idea to address the lack of affordable health monitoring equipments required by doctors in developing countries through the introduction of a prototype device which is able to observe 5 vital signs.
The development of this MedTech product received commendation and recognition from multiple startup mentors, in particular Mentorloop, Outcome.Life Start-Up Victoria, and Melbourne Accelerator Program (MAP).

Intel-MASCA Business Case Competition 2016 - 1st Place Winner
Intel Microelectronics (M) Sdn. Bhd.
April 2016
In this state level business case competition, we were required to provide recommendations for Intel to gain market share in the cloud computing industry within the Asia Pacific region.
Cooperating as a team of 5, our proposal was to incorporate and integrate cloud computing with Intel servers in China's energy industry with a focus on data visualisation and power redistribution in the national grid. This allows for accurate and effective real-time supply computation and demand prediction to increase efficiency in power transmission.

Corporate Insight and Empowerment (CIE) Case Study Challenge 2015 - 1st Place Winner
Malaysian Students' Council of Australia (MASCA)
September 2015
As a participant in the Engineering Division of this state level case study competition, we worked as a team of 4 to design and pitch an Internet-of-Things (IoT) based solution to a major problem facing a metropolis, particularly in the aspect of Energy Efficiency.
The solution suggested was an electromechanical switch relay paired with WiFi technology on a smartphone to control electrical appliances in a room. At a higher level, energy efficiency is improved by energy usage monitoring utilising smart power meters of a building and a custom made app.
